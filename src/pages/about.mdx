# About this site

**Scrimba Guide** is an independent reader's guide to [Scrimba](https://scrimba.com/?via=u42d4986), the interactive coding platform. It exists because Scrimba's catalog is sprawling (70-plus courses, four career paths, dozens of one-off projects, pricing that varies by region) and the official site is built to sell, not to help you decide.

## Who writes this

Scrimba Guide is written and maintained by **[Yassine El Haddad](https://www.linkedin.com/in/yassine-el-haddad/)**, a software and AI engineer based in Morocco.

He builds production AI systems for a living: retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines, search and retrieval systems, and the automation that ties them together, mostly in Python, TypeScript, and Go. The nightly scraper that keeps this site's Scrimba catalog accurate is his own work, built with the same tools he uses professionally.

He also learned to code outside a traditional computer-science degree, through the project-based, peer-driven [42 Network](https://www.42network.org/). That is the point of view behind this site: someone who works with these skills daily and can judge which course content actually transfers to real engineering work, and who also remembers what it is like to learn to code without a formal program, which is the position most Scrimba learners are in.

You can verify who he is on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/yassine-el-haddad/) and [GitHub](https://github.com/yel-hadd).

## How this site is researched

The catalog data here is not assembled by hand. A nightly Python crawler ([`scraper/scrape.py`](https://github.com/yel-hadd/scrimbaguide.tech/blob/main/scraper/scrape.py)) reads Scrimba's public course pages, extracts the embedded JSON-LD structured data, and writes it to a normalized dataset that the course pages are generated from. On top of that data, each guide draws on:

- **Instructor talks and conference recordings** for the people who teach the major paths (Bob Ziroll, Per Borgen, Kevin Powell, Tom Chant), which tell you a lot about what their lessons feel like.
- **Reddit, Discord, and forum threads** where actual learners discuss their wins and frustrations.
- **Third-party reviews** on Trustpilot, Course Report, and developer blogs, cited and linked rather than summarized.

## How the site stays honest

- **Pricing.** This site never quotes a Scrimba price. They vary by country, change without notice, and the [official page](https://scrimba.com/our-pricing) has the live number. Every reference points there.
- **Affiliate disclosure.** Some links to Scrimba carry an affiliate parameter. If you subscribe through one, this site earns a commission at no extra cost to you. That does not change which free resources get recommended or which competitors get the nod when they fit better.
- **No fabricated quotes.** If a testimonial does not link to a real person, a real Reddit thread, or a real review site, it does not appear here. Earlier versions of these pages used synthesized quotes; those have been removed.
- **Not affiliated.** Scrimba Guide is independent. It is not operated by, or endorsed by, Scrimba.

## What the data can and cannot show

- **Course counts, durations, instructor names, and free vs paid status** come from Scrimba's own structured markup, not from a reading of marketing copy.
- **Removed or renamed courses** show up in the diff the next morning, which is how the site catches quietly retired content.
- **Module breakdowns** are a limitation. Scrimba moved the anonymous module DOM behind login in 2026, so the breakdowns here come from a legacy capture of 31 courses. Newer courses show JSON-LD-level data only, and that gap is flagged on the affected pages.
