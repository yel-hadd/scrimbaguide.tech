## How Scrims Actually Work

A "scrim" is Scrimba's proprietary lesson format. It looks like a video, but it's something entirely different under the hood.

### What You See

An instructor talks and writes code on screen, just like a YouTube tutorial. The difference is invisible until you interact with it.

### What's Actually Happening

Instead of recording pixels (like a regular video), Scrimba records:
- **Keystrokes**, every character the instructor types
- **Cursor movements**, where they click, select, navigate
- **Audio**, their voice explanation
- **File state**, the complete state of all files at every moment

Because the code is stored as text (not as a video frame), it remains **live and editable**.

### What You Can Do

At any point during a lesson, you can:
1. **Pause** the timeline
2. **Click into the code**, it becomes a fully functional editor
3. **Edit anything**, change variables, add functions, delete lines
4. **Run your version**, see the output in the built-in mini-browser
5. **Resume**, the instructor continues from their version; your edits are saved separately

This is fundamentally different from every other coding education platform in existence.

## Why This Matters for Learning

### Active vs Passive Learning

Educational research consistently shows that **active learning** (doing, experimenting, making mistakes) produces 2-3x better retention than passive learning (reading, watching, listening). Scrimba's format makes passive consumption almost impossible, the code is right there, inviting you to interact.

### Zero-Friction Practice

The most common barrier to coding practice is setup time. On YouTube, "following along" requires opening an editor, creating files, installing dependencies. Scrimba eliminates all of this. The editor IS the lesson.

> *"No setup, no switching tabs, no wasting time. This small feature made a huge difference for me, I finally started practicing consistently."*

### The Experimentation Loop

When you can modify the instructor's code with zero risk, you naturally start experimenting:
- "What happens if I change this value?"
- "Can I solve this a different way?"
- "What if I add an extra feature?"

This experimentation builds deeper understanding than following instructions ever could.

While the interactive scrim is the headline feature, reviewers also highlight:

> *"The teachers are awesome... the content is abundant and of excellent quality."*

Scrimba's instructors include specialists like Kevin Powell (CSS), Bob Ziroll (React), and others known for clear, engaging teaching styles.

### Built-In Challenges (37% mention hands-on practice)

After each section, challenges test your understanding without showing the solution first. This is the mechanism that converts watching into doing.

### Community (6.4% mention Discord)

The Discord community (55k+ members) offers code reviews, study groups, and direct access to the Scrimba team, features that matter more than they might seem for maintaining motivation.

Rather than individual courses, Scrimba organizes content into structured career paths that take you from zero to job-ready. This removes the "what should I learn next?" anxiety.
