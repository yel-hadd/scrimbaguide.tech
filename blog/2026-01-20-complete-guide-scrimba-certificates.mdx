## How to Earn a Certificate

You earn a certificate by completing **100% of the lessons** in any of Scrimba's designated Career Paths:

1.  **[Frontend Developer Path](/docs/paths/frontend-developer-path)** (81.6 hrs)
2.  **[Fullstack Developer Path](/docs/paths/fullstack-developer-path)** (108.4 hrs)
3.  **[Backend Developer Path](/docs/paths/backend-developer-path)** (30.1 hrs)
4.  **[AI Engineer Path](/docs/paths/ai-engineer-path)** (11.4 hrs)

### Do individual courses give certificates?
Generally, no. Individual courses (like "Learn React") do not award certificates—only the comprehensive Career Paths do. This ensures that a Scrimba certificate actually carries weight and represents a significant body of work.

## How to Add Your Certificate to LinkedIn

Adding your certificate to your LinkedIn profile is one of the best ways to signal to recruiters that you are actively keeping your skills sharp.

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1.  **Get your link:** On your Scrimba dashboard, open your certificate and click "Share". Copy the public URL.
2.  **Go to LinkedIn:** Navigate to your profile scroll down to the **"Licenses & certifications"** section. (If you don't see it, click "Add profile section" at the top).
3.  **Click (+):** Add a new entry.
4.  **Fill in the details:**
    *   **Name:** "Frontend Developer Career Path" (or whichever path you finished)
    *   **Issuing Organization:** Search for "Scrimba" and select the official company page.
    *   **Issue Date:** The month/year you finished.
    *   **Credential ID:** (Optional) You can leave this blank or use the unique ID at the end of your certificate URL.
    *   **Credential URL:** Paste the link you copied in Step 1.

## Common Issues & Fixes

### "My name is wrong on the certificate!"
Your certificate automatically uses the display name from your Scrimba account settings.
*   **Fix:** Go to Settings > Profile and update your name. The certificate should update automatically.
*   **Still stuck?** If it doesn't update, email `help@scrimba.com` with the subject "Certificate Name Change".

### "I finished the path but didn't get a certificate."
Check your progress bar. It must say **100%**.
*   **Fix:** Sometimes a single 1-minute lesson was skipped. Open the path overview and look for any unchecked circles. You might need to re-watch the final "Outro" video to trigger the completion.

## Are Scrimba Certificates "Recognized"?

We get this question a lot. The honest answer is: **Startups and tech companies care about skills, not accreditation.**

Scrimba is not a university. It is a vocational training platform. However, Scrimba is highly respected in the industry because:
1.  **Transparency:** Anyone can click your certificate link and see exactly what the curriculum covered.
2.  **Project-Based:** Recruiters know Scrimba is hands-on.
3.  **Partnerships:** The specific "Frontend Developer Path" was created in collaboration with **Mozilla MDN**, giving it significant prestige.

**Our Advice:** Treat the certificate as the "cherry on top." The "sundae" is your **Portfolio of Projects**.
