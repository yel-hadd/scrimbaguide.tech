# Scrimba Discount Codes in 2026

<DisclosureNotice />

If you Google "Scrimba discount code" you'll find dozens of coupon sites listing codes like SAVE50, WELCOME20, or SUMMER2024. None of them work. Scrimba does not run a public-coupon program and never has, in the same way Spotify or Netflix don't. The five ways you actually pay less are: annual billing, our affiliate link (which gives 20% off), verified student status, the GitHub Student Developer Pack, and the regional purchasing-power pricing that Scrimba applies automatically.

**Quick answer:** No public coupon codes exist. The legitimate ways to save are: (1) switch to annual billing, (2) use an affiliate partner link like ours for 20% off, (3) email help@scrimba.com from an academic address for the student discount, (4) claim Scrimba via the GitHub Student Pack for one free month, and (5) trust the regional pricing that Scrimba applies automatically based on your real location. Don't use a VPN to spoof location.

*Last reviewed: May 2026. Discount terms and percentages may change. Verify with [Scrimba's help center](https://scrimba.helpscoutdocs.com/) before paying.*

## Why the coupon sites are lying to you

Coupon aggregator sites are SEO businesses. They publish "Scrimba coupon code" pages whether or not there are real codes, because the search volume is there. The codes they list are either expired affiliate codes that the affiliate has since rotated, screenshots fabricated to inflate "verified today" counts, or codes that never existed.

Scrimba's published discount structure is built around eligibility (student, region, GitHub Student Pack) and commitment (annual billing), not promo codes. That's why no SAVE25 or BLACKFRIDAY26 code will work at checkout.

## 1. Annual billing (the easiest discount)

On the [official pricing page](https://scrimba.com/our-pricing), toggle to annual. The per-month rate drops meaningfully versus monthly billing. Scrimba doesn't quote a fixed percentage publicly, and the exact savings vary by region, but annual is almost always the right choice if you'll study for more than three months.

This is the discount everyone qualifies for. Take it.

## 3. Student discount (academic email required)

Scrimba offers a substantial student discount that isn't visible at checkout. You request it by email:

1. Create a free Scrimba account at the email address tied to your academic email (or be ready to verify).
2. Email **help@scrimba.com** from your `.edu`, `.ac.uk`, or equivalent academic email.
3. Subject: "Student discount request."
4. Ask for the current student discount link and confirm what proof they need.

Terms vary and may change. See [Scrimba's student-discount help article](https://scrimbahelp.zendesk.com/) for the live policy.

## 4. GitHub Student Developer Pack (one free month)

If you're already verified through [GitHub Education](https://education.github.com/pack), Scrimba is one of the partners. The current benefit is one free month of Pro.

1. Go to the [GitHub Student Developer Pack offers page](https://education.github.com/pack).
2. Search for Scrimba.
3. Follow the redemption link to claim the month.
4. After the month, the subscription rolls over to the standard rate. Cancel before renewal if you don't want to pay.

This stacks with annual billing if you go annual after the free month, though confirm the current stacking rules with Scrimba support before relying on it.

## 5. Regional purchasing-power pricing (automatic)

Scrimba applies regional pricing automatically. If you live in a country with lower average purchasing power (India, Brazil, Nigeria, Indonesia, and others), the displayed Pro price is significantly lower than the US/UK rate. You don't need a code; just visit the pricing page from your real connection.

**Important:** don't use a VPN to spoof your location. Scrimba's billing system checks against the location on your payment method, and mismatches trigger account bans. There are public Trustpilot reviews and Scrimba support posts confirming this; it's a hard rule.

## What does not work

- **Public coupon codes** like SAVE50, SCRIMBA25, etc. They don't exist.
- **Code generators**. There is no algorithm; codes aren't random strings tied to a server-side validator.
- **Reusing someone else's affiliate or student link** if it's tied to a specific account.
- **Account sharing**. Against the terms of service and breaks your progress tracking anyway.
- **Multiple new-account discounts**. One per person.

## The realistic best-case scenario

Stack what stacks: regional pricing applies automatically (no action needed), then either go through the affiliate link for 20% off or get the student discount via email if you qualify. Then pick annual billing. That's the floor on what you can pay legitimately.

If you're a student with an academic email and the GitHub Student Pack, claim the free month first, decide whether to continue, then go annual through the student channel.

## Refunds (the other "discount")

Worth knowing: Scrimba offers a 7-day refund window on your first purchase, one refund per account, per their [refund policy](https://scrimbahelp.zendesk.com/). If you subscribe and the format doesn't click within a week, request a refund via help@scrimba.com.
