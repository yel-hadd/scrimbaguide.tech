The consistent pattern across [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/www.scrimba.com) (mid-4s out of 5) and r/learnprogramming threads is that the people who renew aren't doing it for the first-year curriculum. They renew because the format and the new-course cadence keep paying back after the original path is finished.

Let's break down what makes them stay.

## Who This Is For

You are deciding whether long-term Pro is worth it and want the reasoning from someone who keeps paying yearly.


## What You Get with Scrimba Pro

For context, here's what the Pro subscription includes:

- **Full access to all career paths**, Frontend (81.6 hrs), Fullstack (108.4 hrs), AI Engineering, and more
- **{totalCoursesLabel} courses** across React, JavaScript, TypeScript, Python, CSS, AI, and backend
- **Solo projects** with design specs for portfolio building
- **Discord community access**, code reviews, study groups, career support channels, weekly Town Hall
- **Certificates** for completed career paths
- **New courses added regularly** as frameworks and technologies evolve

See our [Pro vs Free comparison](/docs/pricing/pro-vs-free) for the full breakdown.

## Why the Value Compounds Over Time

### Year 1: The Foundation

Most users start with a career path, Frontend or Fullstack. This is the primary value proposition: a structured curriculum that takes you from beginner to job-ready. At 10-15 hours per week, this fills most of the first year.

### Year 2+: Continued Learning

This is where Scrimba differentiates itself from platforms you cancel after finishing one course. Because technology evolves, there's always something new to learn:

- **React 19 patterns** replace what you learned about React 18
- **AI engineering courses** cover a field that didn't exist when you started
- **TypeScript** becomes important as your career progresses
- **Backend courses** expand your skillset from frontend to fullstack

Scrimba regularly adds new courses and updates existing ones. The Pro subscription gives you access to all of it, so your learning never stops.

### The Working Developer Use Case

A recurring theme in [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/www.scrimba.com) reviews from working engineers is that the platform doesn't graduate them. They keep returning to learn new frameworks, because the same pause-and-edit loop that worked for beginners works for picking up a new tool quickly.

When you encounter a new framework or technology at work, Scrimba's interactive format lets you learn it faster than documentation or YouTube. The same feature that made it great for beginners, coding inside the lesson, makes it great for experienced developers picking up new tools.

> **If you're considering Pro for year-two learning specifically:** the [AI Engineer Path](/docs/paths/ai-engineer-path) is the most actively expanding track, with new modules shipping roughly quarterly. The <AffiliateLink href="https://scrimba.com/s0v687325e">public demo scrim</AffiliateLink> (no signup) is still the cheapest way to confirm the format works for the way you actually learn.

## The Pricing Reality

Scrimba Pro sits in the low-hundreds-per-year bracket on annual billing, in roughly the same band as Codecademy Pro and well below Frontend Masters. The actual numbers shift by region and promotion, so we don't quote them here. The [official pricing page](https://scrimba.com/our-pricing) shows your regional rate. Compared to a US bootcamp (typically two orders of magnitude more), the recurring decision isn't "is Pro expensive" but "am I using the format enough to justify the line item."

Scrimba also applies **regional purchasing-power pricing automatically**, which is a recurring reason readers in lower-income regions cite for staying subscribed.

For the detailed cost comparison, see our [Scrimba vs Bootcamps pricing analysis](/docs/pricing/scrimba-vs-bootcamps) and the broader [pricing breakdown](/docs/pricing/).

## When Pro Isn't Worth It

To be fair, there are cases where the Pro subscription may not be the right choice:

- **You only need one specific course**, Check if it's available in the [free tier](/blog/best-free-scrimba-courses) first
- **You don't have 5+ hours per week to study**, The value comes from consistent use. If you can only manage an hour per week, the free tier may suffice
- **You're an experienced senior developer**, Scrimba's content targets beginners through mid-level. If you're looking for advanced system design or architecture content, other platforms may serve you better

## What Keeps People Coming Back

Based on review analysis, the top reasons for long-term retention:

1. **The interactive format is unique**, No other platform offers it. Once you learn this way, passive video feels frustrating.
2. **New content keeps appearing**, AI courses, fullstack path, updated React curriculum, there's always something new.
3. **The community is genuine**, The Discord isn't a ghost town. Active code reviews, study groups, and direct access to instructors.
4. **The value-to-cost ratio**, annual billing per month often lands below common streaming services on the regional pricing page, while delivering a structured curriculum rather than entertainment.
