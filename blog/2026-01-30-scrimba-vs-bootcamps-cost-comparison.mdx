# Scrimba vs Coding Bootcamps: The $13,250 Question

<DisclosureNotice />

**Quick Answer:** Average bootcamps cost ~$14,000; Scrimba Bootcamp is $750 for 3 months; Scrimba Pro is ~$39/month. Scrimba is 10–30x cheaper. You trade off job guarantees, employer networks, and immersive structure — but keep flexibility and can work while learning. Many start with Pro to validate commitment at low cost.

*Last reviewed: March 2026. Bootcamp and Scrimba pricing may change — check [Scrimba](https://scrimba.com/?via=u42d4986) for current rates.*

## Who This Is For

Career changers comparing Scrimba to immersive bootcamps, learners who must keep working while studying, or anyone weighing cost vs mentorship and job placement.

But can a $750 platform actually deliver the same outcomes as a $14,000+ program? Let's be honest about what you get and what you trade.

## What Traditional Bootcamps Offer That Scrimba Doesn't

Let's be fair about the trade-offs:

### Job Guarantees

Some bootcamps (like Flatiron and App Academy) offer conditional job guarantees or Income Share Agreements (ISAs) where you don't pay until you're employed. Scrimba does not offer this. You're investing $750 upfront with no employment guarantee.

### Immersive Environment

Full-time bootcamps create an immersive environment where coding is your entire focus for 12-16 weeks. This forced focus works well for people who struggle with self-discipline. Scrimba is self-paced, which requires more internal motivation.

### Employer Networks

Traditional bootcamps often have partnerships with employers who hire directly from their programs. Bootcamp career teams send your resume to partner companies. Scrimba doesn't have formal employer partnerships.

### Live Instruction

Bootcamps feature live instructors you can interrupt with questions. Scrimba's instruction is pre-recorded (though interactive), and help comes through the Discord community.

## What Scrimba Offers That Bootcamps Don't

### Flexibility

You can keep your day job. Study at 6am before work, at 10pm after the kids are asleep, or weekends only. Traditional full-time bootcamps typically require 40-60 hours per week during fixed hours.

### AI Engineering Content

Most traditional bootcamps don't cover AI engineering. Scrimba offers [15+ AI courses](/docs/courses/ai/) including agents, RAG, MCP, and LangChain — skills that are increasingly valuable in the 2026 job market.

### The Interactive Format

Scrimba's format (pause video, edit instructor's code) is genuinely unique. No bootcamp uses this approach. It creates a tighter feedback loop than switching between a video lecture and a separate code editor.

### Lower Risk

If it doesn't work out, you're out $750 — not $14,000. And you can cancel Scrimba Pro monthly with no long-term commitment.

## The Outcomes Question

### Bootcamp Outcomes

According to industry data:
- **79%** of bootcamp graduates are employed in programming jobs
- Average starting salary: **~$69,000**
- Average salary increase over pre-bootcamp earnings: **~$25,000**
- Time to employment after graduation: **3-6 months** on average

### Scrimba Outcomes

Scrimba doesn't publish formal outcome statistics like accredited bootcamps do. However, the [Scrimba Podcast](https://podcast.scrimba.com/) features numerous success stories of career changers who landed developer jobs within 3-6 months of starting. Common timelines from public success stories:

- **Fredrik**: No experience to junior frontend developer in 3 months
- **Kyle**: Business background to frontend developer in 6 months
- **Aldhair**: Cruise ship worker to remote web developer in 5 months

## Our Recommendation

**Choose Scrimba Bootcamp ($750) if:**
- Budget matters — you can't afford $14,000
- You need to keep working while learning
- You're self-motivated and disciplined
- You want AI engineering skills
- You want flexibility in your schedule

**Choose a traditional bootcamp if:**
- You can commit to full-time study for 3-6 months
- You want a job guarantee or employer network
- You need maximum external accountability
- You can afford the tuition (or qualify for an ISA)
- You're in a market where bootcamp brand recognition matters

**The most common winning strategy**: Start with [Scrimba Pro](/docs/pricing/) to test your commitment at low cost. If you love it, continue with the career path. The $39/month investment lets you validate that coding is right for you before committing thousands.
