# Scrimba Certificates & Accreditation: Are They Worth It?

**Is this for you?** You need the truth on **“accredited?”**, **LinkedIn**, and **what hiring managers skim first**.

**Quick answer:** Scrimba paths issue **non-accredited Certificates of Completion** — legit **milestones**, not **degrees**. **Employers care more about what you shipped** than the PDF. **Use the certificate + live projects + GitHub** together; don’t expect a badge to replace a portfolio.

*Last reviewed: 2026-03-30.*

If HR wants a **university brand**, compare **Coursera-style programs** — see our [Scrimba vs Coursera](/docs/comparisons/scrimba-vs-coursera) guide.

## Who this is for

Career changers who want **credibility signals** without confusing **certificate vs competence**.


## What Certificates Does Scrimba Offer?

Scrimba provides **certificates of completion** for its structured career paths:

| Certificate | Path Duration | Access |
|---|---|---|
| Frontend Developer Certificate | 81.6 hrs | Pro |
| Fullstack Developer Certificate | 108.4 hrs | Pro |
| Backend Developer Certificate | 30.1 hrs | Pro |
| AI Engineer Certificate | 11.4 hrs | Pro |

Certificates are awarded after you complete all modules in a path. They include your name, the path completed, and a verification link.

## How to Earn a Scrimba Certificate

1. **Subscribe to Scrimba Pro** — certificates are only available with a Pro subscription
2. **Choose a career path** — pick [Frontend](/docs/paths/frontend-developer-path), [Fullstack](/docs/paths/fullstack-developer-path), [Backend](/docs/paths/backend-developer-path), or [AI Engineer](/docs/paths/ai-engineer-path)
3. **Complete all modules** — watch all screencasts, complete all challenges, and finish all projects
4. **Claim your certificate** — it's automatically generated when you finish the path

## Adding Your Certificate to LinkedIn

1. Go to your LinkedIn profile and click **Add profile section**
2. Select **Licenses & Certifications**
3. In the form, set **Name** to "Frontend Developer Certificate" (or your path name), **Issuing organization** to Scrimba, **Issue date** to your completion date, and **Credential URL** to the verification link from your certificate
4. Save — it now appears on your profile

## Is Scrimba Accredited?

**Scrimba is not a university and does not offer accredited degrees.** Like Codecademy, Udemy, and other online platforms, Scrimba's certificates are non-accredited certificates of completion.

This means:
- Scrimba certificates don't count toward academic credits
- They don't replace a CS degree for roles that require one
- They don't carry the same weight as professional certifications (like AWS or Google Cloud)

However, for most web development and AI engineering roles, accreditation is irrelevant. Hiring managers care about what you can build, not where you studied.

## Do Employers Value Scrimba Certificates?

The honest answer: **employers value your skills and portfolio more than any certificate.** But Scrimba certificates serve several useful purposes:

### What certificates DO help with:
- **Signal commitment** — completing an 81-hour path shows dedication
- **LinkedIn visibility** — recruiters searching for "React" may find your certificate
- **ATS keywords** — some applicant tracking systems scan for certifications
- **Personal motivation** — having a concrete goal helps you finish the curriculum

### What matters more:
- **Your portfolio** — working projects deployed online
- **Your GitHub** — clean code and consistent activity
- **Your ability to talk about what you built** — interview performance
- **Referrals and networking** — Scrimba's Discord community can help here

## Scrimba vs Other Certificates

| Platform | Certificate Type | Employer Recognition |
|---|---|---|
| Scrimba | Path completion | Moderate (web dev focused) |
| Coursera | University-branded | Higher (academic brand value) |
| Codecademy | Course completion | Similar to Scrimba |
| freeCodeCamp | Certification (300+ hrs each) | Moderate (widely known) |
| AWS / Google Cloud | Professional certification | High (industry standard) |

If academic credentialing is your primary goal, Coursera or a university program may be a better fit. If you want to learn practical skills and get hired, Scrimba's approach is more direct.
