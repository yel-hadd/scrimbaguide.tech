# Who Scrimba Is For

**Quick answer.** Scrimba fits five common starting points especially well: complete beginners who want structure over a scattered tutorial playlist, CS students filling the gap between theory and shipped projects, designers moving into front-end code, marketers who want to build and ship without waiting on engineering, and working professionals learning on a few hours a week. Below is an honest read on where each background fits, and where it does not. We are an independent reviewer, not Scrimba, so the caveats are real.

## Find your starting point

- **[Scrimba for absolute beginners](/docs/for/beginners/)**: Never written a line of code. A zero-intimidation first 30 days, with free courses to validate fit before you pay.
- **[Scrimba for CS students](/docs/for/cs-students/)**: You have theory and algorithms but little shipped on the web. Where Scrimba closes the practical gap, and a tighter timeline.
- **[Scrimba for designers](/docs/for/designers/)**: Moving from design into front-end code. HTML, CSS, and React without losing the design sensibility you already have.
- **[Scrimba for marketers](/docs/for/marketers/)**: Build landing pages, ship experiments, and stop waiting on engineering. What is worth learning and what to skip.
- **[Scrimba for busy professionals](/docs/for/busy-professionals/)**: Learning on 5 to 15 hours a week around a full-time job. Realistic schedules and how to keep momentum.
