# Scrimba vs Coding Bootcamps

<DisclosureNotice />

Traditional bootcamps average $10,000 to $20,000 (some run as high as $24,000). Scrimba Pro is a subscription at roughly two orders of magnitude less; see <AffiliateLink href="https://scrimba.com/our-pricing">scrimba.com/our-pricing</AffiliateLink> for the current rate in your region. You trade off live instruction, employer networks, and job guarantees, but keep flexibility and can work while learning.

That is the price floor for a few months of full-time instruction with career services.

### Read the job-guarantee fine print

A "job guarantee" is rarely the blanket promise it sounds like. Across the programs above and most others, the refund is conditional on things like holding a bachelor's degree, being authorised to work in the US or Canada, applying to a minimum number of jobs per week, attending every required career session, and accepting any qualifying offer above a salary threshold. Springboard itself reports that only about 1.2% of Software Engineering Career Track students between 2020 and 2022 actually received a guarantee refund, which tells you how rarely the clause pays out in practice. The guarantee is real, but it is a backstop with conditions, not a free pass. When you compare it against Scrimba's honest "no guarantee," you are really comparing a conditional refund you will probably never claim against a far lower price you pay regardless of outcome.

## What You Trade Off

### With Scrimba

**Advantages:**
- Keep your day job (self-paced and flexible)
- 10 to 30 times cheaper than traditional bootcamps
- Access to the latest AI engineering content
- Interactive format superior to traditional video lectures

**Trade-offs:**
- No job guarantee
- Requires more self-discipline than an immersive program
- Less intensive mentorship than full-time bootcamps
- Career support is community-driven rather than dedicated coaches

### With Traditional Bootcamps

**Advantages:**
- Immersive full-time environment forces focus
- Some offer job guarantees or deferred tuition (ISAs)
- Dedicated career coaches and employer partnerships
- Around 79% of alumni employed full-time and an average starting salary near $69,000, per [Course Report's alumni outcomes survey](https://www.coursereport.com/coding-bootcamp-cost-comparison) (most land that first job within roughly six months)

**Trade-offs:**
- Cannot work during full-time programs
- $10,000 to $24,000 upfront cost (or ISA debt)
- Fixed schedule, miss a week and you fall behind
- Quality varies dramatically between programs

## Informal Scrimba timelines (community)

Scrimba does not publish audited outcome stats like many bootcamps. For directional context, the [Scrimba Podcast](https://podcast.scrimba.com/) and public learner stories often cite **roughly 3–6 months** from start to first developer role, e.g. career changers moving from no web experience or unrelated jobs into junior frontend roles. Treat these as **anecdotes**, not guarantees; your mileage depends on hours per week, market, and portfolio quality.

## Who Should Choose What

**Choose Scrimba Pro if:**
- You're self-motivated and disciplined
- You need to keep working while learning
- Budget is a primary concern
- You want flexibility to learn at your own pace
- You're interested in AI engineering (most bootcamps don't cover this)

**Choose Scrimba Bootcamp if:**
- You want structured mentorship at a fraction of bootcamp cost
- You benefit from cohort-based accountability
- You want the best value combination of guidance and affordability

**Choose a Traditional Bootcamp if:**
- You can commit to full-time study for 3-6 months
- You want a job guarantee or employer network
- You need maximum external structure and accountability
- Budget is not the primary concern
